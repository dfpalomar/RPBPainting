Gallery Design Guidelines

📐 Image Ratios

Use 16:10 or 4:3 for uniform grids. Masonry layout can use natural photo ratios but avoid anything taller than 2:1 or wider than 3:1 to keep the rhythm balanced.

📸 Photography Style

Shoot finished spaces with natural daylight. Wide-angle shots that show the whole room. Avoid close-ups of paint cans or rollers — focus on the result, not the process. Consistent white balance across the set.

🏷️ Project Metadata

Every gallery item must include: project name, location, category (Residential / Commercial / Specialty / Exterior / Venue), and optionally: duration, number of rooms, and paint brand used.

✨ Hover & Interaction

Use a bottom-gradient overlay on hover (never a flat colour). Reveal project title, location, and category tag. Include a subtle scale transform (1.5–2%) and the arrow icon top-right for clickability.

🎯 Layout Recommendations

Homepage: Use Layout B (Featured + Grid) with 3 projects max. Portfolio page: Use Layout A (Masonry) as primary with filter bar. Individual project pages: Use Layout C (Uniform Grid) with Before/After slider at the top.

🌙 Lightbox Behaviour

Dark overlay at 95% opacity. Image should not exceed 80% of viewport width. Include keyboard navigation (arrow keys, Escape to close). Dot pagination for multi-photo projects. Swipe support on mobile.

09

Usage Guidelines

Key principles to maintain consistency and quality across all pages.

✓ Do

Use generous whitespace between sections (80–96px). Let content breathe like an Apple product page. Use Wine as the primary CTA colour. Pair Playfair Display headlines with DM Sans body text. Keep navigation simple — max 5 links. Use solid colour backgrounds and let photography carry visual interest. Default to the warm Canvas (#FAF9F6) background.

✗ Don't

Don't use more than two signature colours per section. Avoid heavy drop shadows or hard borders. Don't mix too many type weights — stick to 300, 400, 500, 600. Never use the full 10-step colour scale on one page — pick 3–4 stops per colour. Don't use decorative gradients — keep colours solid and purposeful. Avoid stock photos of paint rollers — favour finished spaces.

10

Design Principles

The philosophy behind every pixel.

✦

Solid, Not Flashy

Every colour earns its place through depth and richness. No gradients, no tricks — just confident, assured colour choices.

◯

Breathe

Generous whitespace is a feature. Let content float in space like an Apple product page. Restraint communicates luxury.

→

Effortless Navigation

Users should find what they need in two clicks or less. Simplicity is king. No clutter, no distraction.

♦

Craft & Authority

Every detail communicates mastery. The design itself should feel like a tailored suit — precise, deliberate, premium.
